Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 4, 2026

Calimetrics (“Calimetrics,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information when you use calimetrics.app, our mobile application, and related services (collectively, the “Service”).

This Policy is intended to reflect applicable privacy requirements, including Quebec’s Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector, as amended by Law 25.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Account information: such as your name, email address, login credentials, and authentication-related information.
  • Third-party sign-in data: if you choose to log in with Google, Facebook, Apple, or another provider, we may receive basic profile information authorized by that provider.
  • Fitness and usage information: such as workout entries, routines, exercise logs, preferences, and other information you choose to enter into the Service.
  • Billing and transaction information: such as subscription status, purchase details, billing country, and payment-related records. Payments are processed by third-party payment providers such as Stripe, and we do not store your full payment card number.
  • Technical and device information: such as device type, operating system, browser type, app version, IP address, and basic diagnostic information.
  • Analytics and tracking information: if enabled with your consent where required, we may collect information about how you interact with the Service using analytics tools, cookies, or similar technologies.

2. How We Use Personal Information

We may use personal information to:

  • Create, authenticate, and manage your account.
  • Provide workout tracking and other core Service functionality.
  • Process subscriptions and maintain billing records.
  • Maintain security, prevent fraud, and detect misuse of the Service.
  • Provide customer support and respond to requests.
  • Improve, analyze, and develop the Service.
  • Send transactional messages, service-related notices, and important updates.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements.

3. Consent

Where required by law, we obtain consent for the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information. Consent requests are intended to be clear, specific, and separate from unrelated terms.

For optional tracking technologies, analytics cookies, or similar tools that are not strictly necessary to provide the Service, we will request your explicit opt-in consent where required before activating them. You can withdraw consent for optional tracking at any time, subject to legal or technical limitations.

4. Minors

Our Service is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 13 without appropriate authorization, we will delete it as required by applicable law.

If a user is a minor under 14 and Quebec law applies, consent for the collection of personal information must be given by the person having parental authority or by the tutor, unless the collection is clearly for the minor’s benefit.

5. How We Share Personal Information

We do not sell personal information. We may share personal information with trusted service providers who help us operate the Service, including:

  • Payment processors, such as Stripe.
  • Hosting, infrastructure, and cloud service providers.
  • Analytics and diagnostic providers, where enabled in accordance with applicable consent requirements.
  • Professional advisors and authorities where required by law or to protect our rights.

These third parties are authorized to use personal information only as necessary to provide services to us, comply with legal obligations, or protect the Service.

6. Privacy by Default

We aim to apply privacy-protective settings by default and limit collection, access, and retention to what is reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Policy.

7. Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Policy, including to maintain your account, provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

When personal information is no longer required, we will delete it or anonymize it, subject to legal retention requirements.

8. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or disclosure. However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your location and the laws that apply, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Withdraw consent, subject to legal or contractual restrictions.
  • Request deletion of personal information, where applicable.
  • Request information about how your personal information has been used or disclosed.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Privacy Officer using the details below.

10. Cross-Border Processing

Some service providers we use may process or store personal information outside Quebec or outside Canada. Where required, we will assess such processing and take reasonable steps to ensure the information receives appropriate protection.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will post the revised version in the Service and update the “Last Updated” date.
